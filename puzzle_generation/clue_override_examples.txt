# Human clue overrides: answer word + original (rejected) clue + preferred clue + reason the original was rejected.
# The goal here is to provide some examples of good and bad clues to improve the quality of new clues.

Word: MEOWS
Original (rejected) clue: Sounds from a litter?
Preferred clue: Cat sounds
Reason original was rejected: litter doesn't necessarily point to cats (could be dogs or other animals) + question mark makes no sense since most people's first instinct is not to think of something else when thinking of 'a litter'

Word: GRACED
Original (rejected) clue: Was on the cover of
Preferred clue: Honored (by someone's presence)
Reason original was rejected: Grammar is wrong, you can't substitue here since "He graced the cover of the magazine" would be replaced by "He was on the cover of the cover of the magazine" which makes no sense. This would only work if "he graced the magazine" was a common phrase.

Reason original was rejected: too difficult, niche baseball clue with no hint like a '?' that the clue refers to baseball. Also "perhaps" implies that being safe at home is an example of "slid" which it is not necessarily since sliding does not guarantee safety.
